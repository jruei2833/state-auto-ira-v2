Dear Records Custodian:

Pursuant to the California Public Records Act, codified at Government Code §§ 7920.000 *et seq.*, the America First Policy Institute (AFPI) requests the following records maintained by the CalSavers Retirement Savings Investment Board ("the Board"):

## Records requested

1. **The list of California employers who have certified exemption from CalSavers** on the basis that the employer offers, sponsors, or otherwise provides a qualified retirement plan to its employees. For each such employer, please provide:

    a. Employer's legal business name
    b. Employer Identification Number (EIN / FEIN), or any other federal or state identifier the Board collects
    c. Employer's primary business address as reported to the Board
    d. The date on which the exemption certification was filed with the Board
    e. The grounds claimed for exemption (i.e., the type of qualifying plan; for example: 401(k), SEP IRA, SIMPLE IRA, defined benefit plan, etc.)
    f. Where available, the date the qualifying plan was established or became effective

2. **Aggregate counts** of exemption certifications received by the Board, broken down by:

    a. Calendar year of the exemption filing (2017–present)
    b. Type of qualifying plan claimed
    c. (If routinely tabulated) Industry or NAICS code of the certifying employer

3. **Any policy or procedural documents** that describe how the Board verifies, audits, or follows up on exemption certifications — for example: whether the Board independently confirms the existence of a qualifying plan, or relies on employer self-certification.

## Format and delivery

We request the records in **electronic, machine-readable format** (CSV, Excel, or comparable structured format) where the Board maintains them in such a form. If only paper or scanned-image versions are available, please notify us and we will discuss options.

We are happy to receive records via email at jruei@americafirstpolicy.com, via secure file-transfer link, or via mail to the address above. We will accept records on a rolling basis if the Board prefers to release them in batches.

## Fee waiver

We request a fee waiver under the public-interest provisions of the CPRA. AFPI is a 501(c)(3) educational and research nonprofit, and the records sought will be used solely for non-commercial public-policy research on the effects of state-facilitated retirement programs. Any analysis we publish will be available to the public free of charge, including to the Board and to California policymakers.

If a fee waiver is not granted, please notify us in advance of any anticipated charge under Gov't Code § 7922.530, including the basis for the charge, before producing records that would incur a fee.

## Statutory response

We respectfully ask that the Board acknowledge receipt of this request and provide an initial determination within ten (10) days as required by Gov't Code § 7922.535. If the Board determines that any portion of the request requires additional time under § 7922.535(b), we ask for written notice of that extension and an estimate of the date by which records will be produced.

If any portion of the request is denied, we request a written statement of the legal basis for denial under Gov't Code § 7922.000 *et seq.*, identifying the specific exemption(s) relied upon and the records or portions of records withheld.

## Background and statement of public interest

This request is made in connection with research AFPI is conducting on the design and effects of state auto-IRA programs nationwide. We have identified, from federal Form 5500 filings, approximately 73,000 California employers that established new private 401(k) plans after CalSavers took effect. The Board's exemption-filing records are the only complementary state-administrative source against which our federal-data identification can be independently validated. Disclosure of these records will materially advance public understanding of how California employers have responded to the CalSavers mandate — a question of direct interest to California policymakers, federal policymakers, and the broader retirement-policy research community.
